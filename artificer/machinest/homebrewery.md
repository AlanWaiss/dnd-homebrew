# Machinist

Machinists are experts at creating specialized weapons and tools to produce almost magical effects. A Machinist’s tools become an extension of the Machinist. They are linked to the artificer like extra limbs.

## Level 3: Tool Proficiency
You gain proficiency with Smith’s Tools. If you already have this proficiency, you gain proficiency with one other type of Artisan’s Tools of your choice.

In addition, when you craft an ordinary or magic weapon, the amount of time required to craft it is halved.

## Level 3: Machinist Spells
When you reach an Artificer level specified in the Machinist Spells table, you thereafter always have the listed spells prepared.

## Level 3: Battle Ready

Your combat training and your experiments with magic have paid off in two ways:

***Arcane Empowerment.*** When you attack with a magic weapon, you can use your Intelligence modifier, instead of Strength or Dexterity modifier, for the attack and damage rolls.

***Weapon Knowledge.*** You gain proficiency with martial weapons.

## Level 3: Tools
Your tinkering has produced some remarkable tools. Your tools can be used as a Spellcasting Focus and count as magical items that are specially tuned to be usable only by you. These tools are fueled by a magical energy that you produce. This energy is represented by Tool Points.

You have Tool Points equal to twice your Proficiency Bonus. You can't have more Tool Points than this number. You regain all expended Tool Points when you finish a Long Rest.

You can use your Tool Points to fuel the options below, along with additional features that use these points.

***Converting Spell Slots to Tool Points.*** You can expend a spell slot to gain a number of Tool Points equal to the slot’s level (no action required).

***Autocrossbow.*** As an action, you can spend 1 Tool Point to spray a hail of bolts in a 60 foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC, taking Piercing damage equal to 1d8 plus your Intelligence modifier on a failed save. Creatures more than 30 feet from you have Advantage on this save.

***Bioblaster.*** As an action, you can spend 1 Tool Point to release a poisonous cloud. Each creature in a 15-foot Cube originating from you makes a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 1d10 Poison damage and has the Poisoned condition until the end of your next turn. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage only.

***Noiseblaster.*** As an action, you can spend 2 Tool Points to emit a loud noice in a frequency that disrupts the reasoning of nearby creatures. Each creature in a 10-foot Emanation originating from you succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC, or the creature becomes confused for 1 minute. Creatures that cannot hear or immune to the Charmed condition automatically succeed on this saving throw. While confused, a creature cannot take Bonus Actions or Reactions and must roll 1d10 at the start of each of its turns to determine its behavior for that turn, consulting the table below.

| 1d10 | Behavior for the Turn |
|:-------|:------|
| 1 | The target doesn’t take an action, and it uses all its movement to move. Roll 1d4 for the direction: 1, north; 2, east; 3, south; or 4, west. |
| 2–6 |	The target doesn’t move or take actions.
| 7–8 |	The target doesn’t move, and it takes the Attack action to make one melee attack against a random creature within reach. If none are within reach, the target takes no action.
| 9–10 | The target chooses its behavior.

At the end of each of its turns, an affected target repeats the save, ending the spell on itself on a success. The effect also ends if the creature takes any damage or if another creature uses its action to shake the affected creature.

## Level 5: Extra Attack
You can attack twice instead of once whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

## Level 5: Additional Tools
You gain access to the following additional tools:

***Drill.*** As a Bonus Action, you can spend 2 Tool Points to activate your Drill for 1 minute. While active, it counts as a simple melee weapon with which you are proficient. It deals 2d8 Piercing damage on a hit and the target’s AC is reduced by 1 for one hour. The effect stacks to a maximum penalty of -3.

***Flash.*** As an action, you spend 2 tool points to release a blinding light. Each creature in a 30-foot Cone makes a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 3d6 Radiant damage and has the Blinded condition until the end of your next turn. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage only.

## Level 9: Tool Recharge
You've learned to recharge your tools on the fly. When you finish a Short Rest, you can regain expended Tool Points, but no more than a number equal to your Proficiency Bonus. Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

## Level 9: Tool Savant
Your Tools gain the following benefits:

***Enhanced Weaponry.*** You roll an additional die when determining the damage of your Autocrossbow, Bioblaster, and Flash.

***Chainsaw.*** You can use a Bonus Action and spend 3 Tool Points to activate your Chainsaw for 1 minute. While active, it counts as a simple melee weapon with which you are proficient. It deals 3d6 Force damage on a hit.

**Dismember.** When you score a Critical Hit with it against a creature, the target makes a Constitution saving throw. On a successful save, roll an additional 2d6 when determining the extra damage. On a failed save, the target’s hit points are reduced to 0. Creatures with a CR higher than your Artificer level or that have legendary resistances automatically succeed on their saving throw. Oozes and targets without a corporeal body such as ghosts are immune to this effect.

***Debilitator.*** This device makes a target Vulnerable to a random damage type. As a Bonus Action, you can spend 3 tool points to target a creature within 30 feet. The target must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, roll 1d10 and consult the table below. The target becomes Vulnerable to the specified damage type for 1 minute.

| 1d10 | Damage Type
|:-------:|:------
| 1 | Acid
2 | Cold
3 | Fire
4 | Force
5 | Lightning
6 | Necrotic
7 | Poison
8 | Psychic
9 | Radiant
10 | Thunder

## Level 15: Air Anchor
When you take the Attack action and hit a creature with a magic weapon, you can use a Bonus Action and spend 4 Tool Points to activate your Air Anchor which will seek out and attach itself to the target of your attack. It will remain attached for a number of days equal to your Artificer level. The Air Anchor is harmless unless the creature makes an attack. If the creature makes an attack roll while the Air Anchor is attached, hit or miss, the Air Anchor will explode after the attack is resolved.

When the Air Anchor explodes, the target must make a Constitution saving throw, taking 10d10 Necrotic damage on a failed save or half as much damage on a successful one.

You can have only one creature under the effect of this feature at a time. As an action, you can remove an unexploded Air Anchor without a check. If you recover your Air Anchor before it explodes, 2 of the Tool Points are refunded.

An intelligent creature can attempt to remove the Air Anchor. To do so, the creature must use an action to make an Intelligence (Arcana) or a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check against your spell DC. On a success, the device is removed. If the check fails by 5 or more, the creature attached to the Air Anchor must make a saving throw as if the creature attacked. If another creature is attempting to remove the device and the creature is within 5 feet of the device, that creature must make also a Dexterity saving throw. The creature takes 3d10 Necrotic and 3d10 Fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
