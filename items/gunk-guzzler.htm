These oversized, charcoal-gray leeches are harvested from the stagnant pools of the deep marshes. The lizardfolk keep them in stone jars filled with a thick, glowing algae slurry that smells like overripe melons and swamp gas.

As a [action]Utilize[/action] action, you can remove the leech from the jar and apply it to a willing, adjacent creature, which becomes its host. The host’s Hit Point maximum is reduced by 1d6 while the leech is attached. At the end of every hour while the leech is attached, roll 1d4. On a roll of 4, the leech casts the [spells]Remove Curse[/spells] spell, targeting its host. Once the spell has been cast from the leech, it cannot do so again until 24 hours have passed. The Gunk-Guzzler can be removed with a [action]Utilize[/action] action from the host or an adjacent creature. The reduction to the host’s Hit Point maximum from the leech is restored when it is removed.

The Gunk-Guzzler is considered a magical object and is not a creature. It has a speed of 1 foot and a swim speed of 5 feet. It has AC 10 and 4 hit points. If the leech drops to 0 hit points, it is destroyed. It loses 1 hit point every hour it is not attached to a host or in its preservation jar. If it is submerged in water, the deterioration is slowed to 1 hit point per day. It regains 1 hit point every hour it is attached to a host or in its preservation jar. When it is reduced to 1 hit point from deterioration, the leech will contract its body and enter a dormant state, surviving for up to 1 month, but if it does so, it takes 24 hours in the preservation jar to recover 1 hit point.

Sentience. The Gunk-Guzzler is a sentient unaligned item with an Intelligence of 1, a Wisdom of 6, and a Charisma of 1. It has hearing and [sense]Blindsight[/sense] out to a range of 60 feet. It can’t speak or read.

Personality. The Gunk-Guzzler’s only concern is feeding. If it is not attached to a host or in its preservation jar, it will seek out the nearest creature and begin feeding as soon as possible.

Preservation Jar. The Gunk-Guzzler is typically kept in a magical preservation jar, filled with a magical slurry that sustains the leech. If it is removed from the jar, it will begin to deteriorate and lose hit points over time.
