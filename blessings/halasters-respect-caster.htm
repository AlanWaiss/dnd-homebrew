You have done the near-impossible: you amused, impressed, or thoroughly confused the Mad Mage of Undermountain enough that he hasn't killed you. Instead, he has woven a fragment of his chaotic arcane weave directly into your soul.

This blessing manifests as a faint, glowing green rune somewhere on your body that occasionally shifts shape or whispers terrible magic puns only you can hear.

The Mad Mage's Favor. You gain a +1 bonus to spell attack rolls and your spell save DC.

The Spark of Madness. This blessing has 3 charges and regains all charges daily at dawn. When you cast a spell of level 1 or higher, you can expend 1 charge to invoke Halaster's chaotic genius. Roll 1d6 to determine how his "respect" manifests:

d6 Effect
1 "He's Laughing With You, Right?" The spell fails, and the spell slot is lost. However, a spectral image of Halaster appears, laughs hysterically, and casts [spells]Tasha’s Hideous Laughter[/spells] (DC 16) on the target of your original spell. If the spell effects an area, choose one creature in the area.
2-3 Arcane Efficiency. The spell is cast normally, but you immediately regain the expended spell slot.
4-5 Overchannel. The spell is cast as if using a spell slot one level higher than the one expended.
6 Unbridled Chaos. The spell takes effect, and you immediately roll on the Wild Magic Surge table. Additionally, you regain all expended sorcery points. If you don’t have sorcery points, you instead gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your level.
