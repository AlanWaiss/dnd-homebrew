Description

Among adventurers, tales are often swapped of rare, elusive creatures that appear unexpectedly in dungeon depths, not to fight, but to plunder. Known as Treasure Goblins, these small, agile humanoids are easily identifiable by the enormous, clattering sacks of wealth they carry on their backs, far larger than their own bodies. When spotted, they don’t defend themselves; they simply giggle maniacally and flee, leading greedy pursuers on a frantic chase through dangerous corridors and straight into other monsters.

The Unreachable Vault

Treasure Goblins are not native to the Material Plane, nor any recognized Outer Plane. They hail from a shadowy, shifting demiplane known only as The Vault. Practically nothing is known about this realm, and scholars debate whether it is a physical location or a pocket dimension existing solely within the extradimensional space of the goblins’ packs.

What is known is their behavior: they appear randomly, often drawn to areas with high concentrations of unexplored treasure. With uncanny speed and single-minded dedication, they scoop up gold, gems, and artifacts, paying no heed to danger or the owners of the treasure.

Hoard-Maw

To the horror of those who examine them closely, a Treasure Goblin’s massive sack is not made of canvas or leather—it is an extension of its own body. This specialized organ, often referred to by planar scholars as a Hoard-Maw or Gilded Gland, is made of an ultra-durable, leathery flap of skin.

The goblin crams gold, gems, and weapons directly down its unnaturally wide throat or into an elastic opening at the top of the gland along its back. Once inside, the goblin's unique biology suppresses the weight of the items, allowing it to sprint at top speeds even while carrying hundreds of pounds of solid gold. When the goblin returns to The Vault, it regurgitates or expels the wealth to add to the plane's eternal pile.

Because the pouch is a living organ holding immense, compressed physical mass under high biological pressure, killing the goblin is catastrophic. The sudden cessation of the creature's magic causes the organ to violently rupture, turning the goblin into a literal, flesh-and-coin explosion.
