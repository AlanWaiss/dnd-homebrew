Traits

Keen Senses. The spirit cat has advantage on Wisdom ([skill]Perception[/skill]) checks.

Mystic Conduit. When a spellcaster prepares spells while the spirit cat is lying on their lap, that spellcaster can choose two level 1 spells and one level 2 spell that it is able to cast and imbue the spirit cat with the spells. The spellcaster doesn’t expend spell slots to imbue the cat in this way. The cat can be imbued with no more than three spells at a time. The cat can use the Spellcasting action to cast each spell once, requiring no material components, and using its own DC and spellcasting ability. Once the spells are expended, the spirit cat must finish a Long Rest before it can be imbued with spells again.

Overstimulation Agression. The spirit cat has Advantage on attack rolls against any creature petting or holding it.

Watching Ghosts. The cat can see 10 feet into the Ethereal Plane when it is on the Material Plane.

Fascinating Purr. Wisdom Saving Throw: DC 14, one Humanoid creature within 30 feet that can hear the cat. Failure: the target has the [condition]Charmed[/condition] condition. While charmed by the spirit cat, the target has the [condition]incapacitated[/condition] condition and must move on its turn toward the cat by the most direct route, trying to get within 5 feet of the cat and pet or hold it. The target doesn’t avoid [action]Opportunity Attack;Opportunity Attacks[/action], but before moving into damaging terrain, such as lava or a pit, and whenever it takes damage from a source other than the cat, the target can repeat the saving throw. It also repeats the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. After 1 minute, it succeeds automatically. If a creature’s saving throw is successful, the creature is immune to the spirit cat’s Fascinating Purr for 24 hours.
